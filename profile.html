My Story:

I studied business at Temple University and graduated with a dual major of Finance and Risk Management. I worked in the financial sector for ten years before attempting to take my education to the next level. I studied part time at LaSalle University in order to earn an MBA.

After 12 years, I felt the need for my next challenge in life. I pursued a new career in financial services. I felt confident that with my large network and strong financial background, that I would be success. I realized quickly that I was not a salesman. Although I did not want to accept failure, I was forced to after several months of ineffectiveness.

I took a seasonal accounting position while I tried to decide what to do with the rest of my life. I was convinced by a close friend that I needed to explore the field of information technology. I attended a Java boot camp with Mindteck Academy and then decided to enter the web development boot camp at NYCDA.

I hope that I can transition into this new field while being able to leverage my experience in finance, accounting, and insurance.
